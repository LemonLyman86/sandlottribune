TEX Cleared For Sale

The Texas Rangers franchise has cleared a major legal and procedural hurdle in its anticipated ownership transfer, raising the likelihood that the club will relocate to a Western Division market as early as the 2027 expansion cycle.

By Joel Sherman  •  The Sandlot Tribune  •  March 7, 2026

SANDLOT DYNASTY LEAGUE, March 7 — After months of behind-the-scenes maneuvering, the Texas Rangers franchise owned by Nate Lees has officially cleared the final procedural hurdle necessary for a league-sanctioned ownership transfer, multiple sources familiar with the situation confirmed to The Sandlot Tribune on Friday.

The development brings the franchise's relocation west one significant step closer to reality. Lees, who guided the Rangers through two active seasons — a promising debut in 2024 and a respectable 2025 campaign — stepped away from the league at season's end due to scheduling conflicts, leaving the franchise's future uncertain heading into the offseason.

With the sale now proceeding on cleared terms, commissioner-level discussions have reportedly turned to what becomes of the 32 players previously under Texas contracts — all of whom were released to free agency at the start of the 2026 offseason when Lees officially declined to re-enter the league.

What Happens to the TEX Roster?

All 32 Texas contracts were released to free agency prior to the 2026 auction. Several high-value players changed hands at premium prices during the February sale. No TEX assets will transfer to any new ownership group — the slate is clean.

The Texas roster, which included several high-value contracts accumulated during Lees's two-year tenure, created one of the most consequential free agent pools in recent league history. Sources confirm the 2026 offseason auction saw multiple TEX alumni change hands at significant prices as owners scrambled to capitalize on the sudden availability.

In the immediate term, the cleared sale formalizes what has already become apparent on the field: the Western Division now belongs to a new franchise. The Los Angeles Dodgers — owned by Jacob and formally admitted to the league in January — have already begun building in earnest, selecting four keepers and filling out their minor league pipeline in the weeks since their expansion admission was approved.

“The west has been watching. This is not a small thing.” — Source familiar with expansion discussions

Whether the cleared sale results in a formal new franchise eventually entering the Western Division — or simply closes the books on the Lees era permanently — remains to be determined. What is not in question: the Texas Rangers chapter of Sandlot Dynasty League history is over, and a new one has already begun in Los Angeles.

The Sandlot Tribune will continue to track developments as the league moves toward its fifth season.
