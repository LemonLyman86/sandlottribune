Dodgers Make MiLB Selections

Jacob’s expansion franchise announces its full slate of minor league picks, signaling a patient long-term approach to dynasty building from the outset in Los Angeles.

By Jeff Passan  •  The Sandlot Tribune  •  March 10, 2026

SANDLOT DYNASTY LEAGUE, March 10 — The Los Angeles Dodgers, the TSDL’s newest franchise, completed their inaugural MiLB draft selections this week, filling out a minor league roster that hints strongly at the organizational philosophy Jacob intends to build around: upside over safety, projection over polish.

The Dodgers entered their first MiLB draft with four confirmed keepers already in place and one of the largest available auction budgets in the league. Having spent only $27 of their $260 keeper salary cap on their MLB roster, Los Angeles arrives at Opening Day carrying significant financial flexibility — a deliberate posture for an expansion team still assembling its foundation.

LAD 2026 Keeper Snapshot

Cristopher Sanchez, SP — PHI — $20 (Yr 3)

Cade Smith, RP — CLE — $5 (Yr 3)

Hunter Goodman, C — COL — $2 (Yr 2)

Bryce Rainer, SS — DET — $0 (Yr 1 MiLB)

Combined salary: $27 of $260 cap.
FAAB budget: $233 entering auction.

With Bryce Rainer already secured as their lone returning MiLB keeper, the Dodgers used their six open MiLB draft slots to build out the farm system. The full list of selections will be published in the Tribune’s official transaction log once the league’s minor league submission window formally closes.

Sources within the league described the Dodgers’ selections as “heavy on arms and high-ceiling bats,” consistent with an ownership group that appears willing to accept short-term uncertainty in exchange for long-term payoff. The approach mirrors what the Las Vegas Athletics did in their own expansion entry two years ago — and that franchise won a championship in its second season.

“They did not come here to rent a window. They came to build something.” — League observer

The Dodgers are expected to be among the most active bidders in the open FAAB market throughout March. With the auction budget representing one of the league’s largest war chests heading into Opening Day, Los Angeles has every tool available to accelerate its build timeline — if Jacob chooses to deploy them.

Whether he pushes those chips in immediately or continues to stockpile assets for a future window will define the identity of this franchise for years to come. The Sandlot Dynasty League has a new team. The question now is how quickly it becomes a contender.
