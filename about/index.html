About ESTN

From a Google Sheet to a Media Network

The Sandlot Dynasty League began in 2022 as a 14-team fantasy baseball dynasty league built on a deep love for the game and an obsession with the data behind it. In the beginning, tracking the league meant maintaining sprawling Google Sheets — roster snapshots, transaction logs, trade histories, salary tracking, and keeper calculations all living in tab after tab of manually-updated cells.

It worked. For a while.

As the league grew — from 14 teams in its inaugural 2022 season to 20 franchises today, 18 of which are active — the manual process became unsustainable. Tracking keeper salaries required knowing every transaction going back years. Understanding trade history meant digging through folders and screenshots. Writing a season preview meant three hours of research before a single word was typed. Every Monday morning update was a small project in itself.

So We Automated It

The first step was building a proper database. Using Python and SQLite, we imported every transaction, trade, lineup change, roster move, and matchup score from 2022 through the present — over 50,000 records across four full seasons. We connected to the FanTrax API to pull live data: current rosters, weekly scores, standings, and real-time transactions. What once took hours to compile now takes seconds to query.

That database became the foundation for everything. Salary escalation rules, keeper projections, FAAB tracking, contract history, dynasty value rankings, player injury logs — all of it lives in a single, queryable system. The same infrastructure powers both the commissioner’s weekly workflow and the articles you read here.

Then Came the Tribune

The Sandlot Tribune was the league’s first attempt at a real publication. Season previews, analysis pieces, league records — all written and published as standalone articles with real bylines, real stats, and genuine craft. A place to tell the story of the league the way it deserved to be told.

It was a start. But a publication without a home isn’t a publication — it’s just a folder of HTML files.

That’s Where ESTN Came In

The Expanded Sandlot Tribune Network was built to give the Tribune the infrastructure it deserved. An ESPN-style portal with live standings, weekly scoreboards, transaction feeds, and admin-controlled content. A proper article reader — Between The Chalk — for the long-form analysis the league earns. A social platform, Rumblr, for the takes and reactions that don’t need 1,000 words. A season events calendar. League records going back to 2022.

Everything you see here — the articles, the standings, the transactions, the social feed — is powered by a data pipeline that updates in a handful of clicks each week. The same system that once required a Sunday afternoon in a spreadsheet now runs in minutes, leaving more time for the thing that matters: actually covering the league.

We’re a fake league with a real infrastructure.
